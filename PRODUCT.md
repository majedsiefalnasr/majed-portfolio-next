# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Startup and product founders (and the product leads around them) who are deciding
whether to bring on a designer for a new product, an app, or a redesign. They land
here from LinkedIn, a referral, or a search, usually skimming on a laptop between
other tabs, sizing up one question: *can this person be trusted with my product?*
They are not browsing for inspiration; they are evaluating a hire.

The job to be done: get from "who is this?" to "I want to talk to this person" in a
few minutes, with enough evidence (real work, a clear way of thinking, results) to
justify reaching out. The primary conversion is a direct outreach / booked chat, so
the contact path and the credibility that leads to it are the whole game.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Majed Sief Alnasr, CX & Product Designer, covering CX/UX/UI,
product design, and consulting. It exists to turn a cold visitor into a warm
conversation: show the work, show the thinking behind it, and make reaching out the
obvious next step. Success is a founder finishing a visit and emailing or booking a
chat, having understood both the caliber of the work and the person behind it.

The site itself is the proof. For a designer, an indecisive or generic portfolio is
disqualifying; the craft of these pages is a live sample of the craft on offer.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, precise. The voice is conversational and human (the page literally
asks the visitor's questions back to them: "Can he help me design something amazing?
👇"), with deliberate emoji and a friendly third-to-first-person shift. Under the
warmth sits rigor: evidence-led case studies, a stated design philosophy, real
results, not vibes. Approachable on the surface, exacting in the details.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel met, not pitched at, and leave confident this
is someone careful, easy to work with, and clearly good at the job.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI/SaaS template.** Cream-bg + tiny tracked eyebrow on every section +
  identical icon-heading-text card grids + hero-metric template. The "AI made that"
  look. The site already commits to a specific warm palette and emoji-led headlines;
  do not let it drift back toward this scaffold.
- **Cold corporate / agency.** Stiff, faceless, navy-and-gray consulting energy with
  no human in it. The personality is the differentiator; never sand it off.
- **Loud Dribbble maximalism.** Over-animated, gradient-heavy, style-over-substance
  showcase where motion and effects bury the actual work. Craft serves the work, not
  the reel.
- **Dense text-heavy résumé.** A wall-of-text CV with no hierarchy, no visual
  storytelling, no craft. This is a portfolio, not a PDF.

## Design Principles

- **The portfolio is the work sample.** Every page is a live demonstration of the
  craft being sold. Indecision, generic patterns, or rough edges read as the
  designer's actual ceiling. Hold the bar here higher than the brief asks.
- **Warm front, precise back.** Lead with personality and approachability; back it
  with evidence. Playfulness earns attention, rigor earns the hire. Never trade one
  for the other.
- **Show, don't tell.** Claims about results, process, and skill are carried by real
  case studies, real metrics, and the quality of the pages themselves, not by
  adjectives.
- **Make the conversation easy.** The whole site bends toward one action: reaching
  out. The contact path stays obvious and low-friction on every page; credibility
  removes the hesitation before it.
- **Distinctive, not loud.** Differentiate through a specific voice and committed
  details, not through volume. The work stays the loudest thing on the page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AAA where it doesn't fight the design, with AA as the firm floor.

- Body text ≥4.5:1 against its background (push toward AAA 7:1 where feasible); large
  text ≥3:1. Watch the muted `--color-body` (#686868) and `--color-subtle` (#999999)
  on tinted backgrounds, the most likely failure points.
- Full keyboard navigation with visible focus; the existing skip-to-content link
  stays. Semantic landmarks and headings.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every animation (the Motion-driven hero,
  reveals, scroll behavior) with a crossfade or instant fallback, not a dead page.
- Don't rely on the pastel accent tones (orange/green/blue/red) alone to carry
  meaning; pair with text or icon so color-blind visitors aren't excluded.
