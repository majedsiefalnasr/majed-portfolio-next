## The quietest interface wins

The best compliment a product can get is silence — no confusion, no support
ticket, no second-guessing. Clarity is invisible by design.

## Three habits

- **Cut before you add.** Every element competes for attention; most don't earn it.
- **Name things honestly.** A label that matches the user's words beats a clever one.
- **Design the edge cases.** Empty, loading, and error states are where trust is won.

> Good design speaks for itself — but only when you've removed everything that
> was speaking over it.
